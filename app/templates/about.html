VibeSearch is a vibe-y search engine for academic articles. It enables you to search using vector search, as well as as 'traditional' and keyword-based search. The idea is that it works only in some journals from the New Media field (list below). It's a prototype.

Search Types

üîÆ Vibe Search (Semantic)

The mystic AI-powered search using ChromaDB and the all-MiniLM-L6-v2 model. It's like having a psychic librarian who understands the ~energy~ of your search. When you ask about "digital art communities", it'll find papers about "online creative collectives" because it gets the vibe, not just the words.

üìö Traditional Search

The practical magic approach! It counts word matches but with a sprinkle of special sauce:

  • Title matches get double points (because titles are important, duh!)
  • Abstract matches get regular points
  • If all your search words appear together? Bonus point! ‚ú®

Like a well-balanced crystal collection - not too woo-woo, not too rigid.

üîç Keyword Search

The "just the facts" option for when you're feeling no-nonsense. No AI magic, no special scoring - just straight-up word matching. Perfect for those Mercury-in-retrograde moments when you need things to be exactly what they say they are.
